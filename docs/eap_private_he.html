3. PIAAC pseudo-cohort

Pseudo-cohort estimates of the share of tertiary attainers educated in private institutions, derived from PIAAC respondent-level data, are being computed by a separate agent. The chart will be added to this slot once that pipeline finishes. Note that PIAAC produces cohort estimates rather than annual time series, so the visualisation will not be directly comparable to sections 1 and 2.

Sources

  • UNESCO UIS OPRI national database - indicators ETOIP.1.PR, ETOIP.2T3.PR, ETOIP.5T8.PR. UIS data browser.
  • PROPHE (Program for Research on Private Higher Education) 2024 country file. prophe.org.
  • EAP membership: World Bank region code EAS for UIS panels; PROPHE region tag EAP for the PROPHE panel.

Methodology notes

  • UIS share private is the percentage of enrolment in private institutions, reported nationally by member states.
  • PROPHE share private is computed from the PROPHE database as private / total tertiary enrolment.
  • Highlighted countries selected for analytic focus on the largest and most widely-discussed EAP systems.

Caveats

  • UIS has visible data gaps for Vietnam and Indonesia at lower ISCED levels; zero or missing reporting in some early years should not be read as zero private provision.
  • PROPHE uses a frozen-year convention for some small countries (MHL, TLS, TON); these are flagged in the hover and rendered with dotted lines and diamond markers.
  • PIAAC will be a pseudo-cohort estimate, not an annual series.
  • Working draft. Numbers should be treated as indicative pending review.
